# Bill 17 — Protect Ontario by Building Faster and Smarter Act, 2025

Reference for the `ontario-land-use-feasibility` skill. Captures the key
legislative amendments from Bill 17 that affect land use planning
feasibility assessments.

**Royal Assent:** June 5, 2025
**Statutes of Ontario 2025, chapter 9
**Source:** https://www.ola.org/en/legislative-business/bills/parliament-44/session-1/bill-17
**Local file:** `documents/provincial/bill-17-2025.html`

## Commencement

- **General rule:** Comes into force on Royal Assent (June 5, 2025)
- **Schedules:** Come into force as provided in each Schedule
- Some provisions come into force on a day named by order of the Lieutenant
  Governor in Council

## Key Amendments Affecting Land Use Planning

### 1. As-of-Right Setback Variations (Planning Act, s. 34(1.4))

**NEW:** A minimum setback distance is deemed to be the prescribed
percentage of the setback distance.

- Province consulting on a prescribed percentage of **up to 10%**
- Example: 5-metre setback -> 4.5 metres permitted as-of-right, no minor variance needed
- **Limited to urban residential lands** (s. 34(1.5))

**Exclusions:**
- Greenbelt Area
- Non-urban-residential parcels
- Land within 300m of a railway
- Land within 120m of conservation authority regulated lands

**Transition provision** (s. 34(1.6)): Deemed setback = that applicable
on the day the provision comes into force.

### 2. Complete Application Requirements Limited

**Problem:** Disagreements over what constitutes a "complete" application
delay the start of statutory decision timelines.

**Changes:**
- New subsections 22(6.0.1), 34(10.3.1), 41(3.5.1), 51(19.0.1), 53(4.0.1)
  Planning Act and 114(23) City of Toronto Act:
  - Information/materials prepared by a **person authorized to practise
    a prescribed profession** are **deemed to meet** applicable requirements
  - Municipal concerns with content go to **merits**, not **completeness**
- New s. 17(21.1): OP amendments adding to complete application
  requirements need **written approval from the Minister**
- Any OPA adopted on or after **May 12, 2025** without ministerial
  approval is **deemed not adopted**

**Proposed regulation (under consultation):**
Excluding from complete application requirements:
- Sun/Shadow impact studies
- Wind impact studies
- Urban Design alignment studies
- Lighting studies

### 3. Development Charges Amendments

- **Long-term care homes exempt** from DCs (new s. 4.4)
- **DC by-law amendments** that decrease charges or remove indexing are
  exempt from the full by-law passing process (ss. 10-18)
- **Non-rental residential DCs**: payable on **occupancy** (not building
  permit issuance); pre-payable without a s. 27 agreement
- **DC freeze cap** (s. 26.2(5.2)): freeze does not apply if total charges
  (including interest) exceed what would be payable without the freeze
- **"Local service" definition**: Province gets regulation-making authority

### 4. Building Code Changes

- New s. 28(6): Building Materials Evaluation Commission cannot exercise
  powers if Canadian Construction Materials Centre (NRC) has examined or
  intends to examine the same material/system/design
- New s. 35(1.1): Clarifies that Municipal Act ss. 9, 10, 11 and City of
  Toronto Act ss. 7, 8 do not authorize municipalities to pass by-laws
  respecting construction or demolition of buildings

## Impact on Feasibility Assessments

1. **Setback flexibility**: Check if the site qualifies for the 10%
   as-of-right setback reduction (urban residential, not in exclusion
   zones). This can turn a "needs minor variance" into "as-of-right".
2. **Complete application scope**: Municipalities cannot demand sun/shadow,
   wind, urban design, or lighting studies as part of a complete application
   (if the proposed regulation is finalized). Affects timeline estimates.
3. **Professional certification**: Reports by prescribed professionals are
   deemed to meet complete application requirements.
4. **DC timing**: For residential projects, DCs are now due on occupancy,
   not building permit.
5. **Ministerial approval for OPAs**: Any OPA that adds to complete
   application requirements needs Minister's approval.
